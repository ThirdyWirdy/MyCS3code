Ending Education Poverty

Imagine being 10 years old and unable to read a simple sentence. It's hard to imagine, but this is the reality for millions of children around the world. Learning poverty is a silent crisis that blocks their future and holds back entire communities. Without this foundational skill, children struggle to learn, grow, and thrive.

Globally, over 250 million children and youth are out of school, and more than 650 million people have only completed primary education. In the Philippines, the situation is especially urgent: 91% of children face learning poverty.

What is Being Done?

Source: Deshan Tennekoon/World Bank (2010)

In order to solve this problem, the World Bank and UNICEF partnered with Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, UK's FCDO, and USAID, launching the Accelerator Program. This program is aimed to help countries globally invest in ending learning poverty, as well as support them in building more resilient education systems. Aside from this, independent organizations like Edu-Cacy and ChildFund also help end learning poverty and poverty as a whole through educational and literacy campaigns.

In the Philippines, programs are also being executed to tackle learning poverty, such as Bawat Bata Makakabasa (BBM) and the Alternative Learning System (ALS). These programs aim to ensure that every child can read, as well as give a second chance to gain literacy and complete basic education. UNICEF is also supporting these efforts by promoting early learning and encouraging parents to play an active role in their children's education. Together, these initiatives are helping build a future where every Filipino child has the opportunity to learn and thrive.

Where we Come In

We also spread awareness about the situation, and ask you, the viewer to join us in our advocacy.
