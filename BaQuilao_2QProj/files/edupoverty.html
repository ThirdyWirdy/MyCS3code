Solving Education Poverty

At Educacy, we believe education is the foundation for breaking the cycle of poverty. Our initiatives focus on providing underserved communities with access to quality learning resources, scholarships, and teacher training programs. But the ones doing an effort to combat this problem are not just us. Other organizations, governments, and foundations have helped with this issue and have given proper education to those who truly need it. Now, below is a showcase of the initiatives and projects we and other organizations have done or are currently doing:

  • In the Gambia, ChildFund and Ding Ding Yiriwa Federation supported teachers and caregivers at two schools where EPW is implemented – Jambanjelly Lower Basic School and Quranic Illumination Center – to find a solution to help children catch up to grade-level literacy standards. The school community agreed to implement remedial classes during the school break that would support 705 children in grades 2-5 improve their literacy, numeracy and social-emotional skills.
  • The Sahel Education Summit founded the Women’s Empowerment and Demographic Dividend. This initiative, founded by Burkina Faso, Chad, Côte d’Ivoire, Mali, Mauritania and Niger, allowed young girls and women who were exposed to factors such as extreme poverty, forced migration, and high fertility rates the opportunity to stay in school.
  • Countries such as Kenya, Uganda, and Tanzania have implemented the Free Education Policy. This policy directly addresses inaccessibility by making school fees low and more affordable for all.
  • NEPC members, NGOs, and research institutes from Western Balkans and Turkey (IPA countries) created the The ARISE – Action for Reducing Inequalities in Education – project. This project will support schools, grassroots organisations and policy makers in developing actions and policies aimed at mitigating the effect of low socio-economic status on students’ achievement, reducing inequalities in education.
  • For Educacy, we have implemented multiple charity programs and helped construct over 100 learning centers in impoverished areas around the world. We have also founded many initatives, like the Education for All (EFA) initative which has provided free education to over 50,000 children in need.
